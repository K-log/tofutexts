How do you prove a tree has no cycles?
You have no nodes with an in-degree of 0.

The root has a n in-degree of 0 and all other nodes have in-degree 1.
You can't have a cyclic graph where all nodes have an in-degree of 1 by using the root(with in-degree 0) because that would contradict the fact that root nodes have an in-degree of 0.

  • A node with out-degree 0 is a leaf.
  • All other nodes are called internal nodes.
  • The depth of the root is 0.
  • The depth of an internal node is 1 + the depth of its parent.
  • The height or the depth of a tree is the depth of the maximum of the depths of all nodes in the tree.
  • A node y is a descendant of x and xz is its ancestor if there is a path from x to y.
  • A minimum common ancestor of two nodes x and y is an ancestor of both and a descendant of all other common ancestors.

Y Combinator:

The Y combinator (fixed-point combinator) is a higher-order function such that:
yf=f(yf)yf = f(yf)yf=f(yf)
It is called a fixed-point because it represents the solution to

x=yfx=fx\begin{aligned} x &= yf\\ x &= fx\\ \end{aligned}xx​=yf=fx​
