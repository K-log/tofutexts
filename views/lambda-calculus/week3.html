Transitivity rule for beta reduction

M=NN=PM=P\frac{M = N \quad N=P}{M = P}M=PM=NN=P​

y̸∈MλM.M=λN.N\frac{y \not\in M}{\lambda M.M = \lambda N.N}λM.M=λN.Ny̸∈M​

Summary of Beta Reduction justifications

  • Lets call a redex of a subexpression an "inner redex".
  • When we show a Beta reduction step with an inner redex we do not explicitly justify that step with a congruence rule.
  • Each step in usual lambda calculus calculation is justified by only the beta rule (or also the eta(η)(\eta)(η) rule if it is includeed in the rules)
  • When we chain together multiple beta reduction steps, we implicity appeal to the transitivity of the -->(beta) reduction relation
  • This process is the way we learned to do equational reasoning in algebra
